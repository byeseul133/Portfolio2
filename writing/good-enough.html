How do you know you are never good enough?
And what's the proof that you are never good enough?

March 4, 2026

I've been struggling my life thinking I'm not good at things. I didn't even start from a neutral place. The baseline was already: I'm not good enough, not smart enough, not skinny enough.

Whatever validation I gave myself never felt real. It was just enough to get through things or to numb it. It never actually landed. So I looked for it outside instead. If someone said I was smart, then I was smart. If someone said I was pretty, then I was pretty. Without realizing it, I was working really hard just to hear those words from people around me, especially the ones closest to me.

The tricky part is I thought I was free from external validation. I wasn't chasing likes on Instagram. I wasn't following the usual timeline. People who know me would probably say I do whatever I want. And I believed that too. But I was still chasing it, just in a quieter way that's much harder to notice.

Sometime this past year, I started to physically feel it. It felt like a mask that went on the moment I walked into a room. I adjusted myself depending on who I was with. Different friends, different situations, different version of me. Like a chameleon. And it was so automatic that I didn't even notice it happening.

I genuinely thought that was just another side of me. Not a mask, not a performance. Just being adaptable. That's what made it so hard to see. You can't put something down if you don't know you're carrying it.

And then I got tired. Not in a dramatic way. Just quietly exhausted.

It wasn't one moment that made me realize all this. What I started feeling this past year was something that had been building for years. Layers of self-doubt, therapy, and people around me slowly helping me see things I couldn't see before.

I remember my first therapist telling me to practice saying "I am good enough." At the time it meant nothing. It just sounded like something you're supposed to say. But somewhere along the way, those same words finally started to land. Not because the words changed, but because I did.

Something shifts when you're no longer in survival mode. You don't need the same protection anymore. And for the first time, I actually wanted to see what was underneath.

Like an egg. You have to crack it open to get to what's inside. But what's inside was always there. It didn't suddenly appear. It was just hidden.

So I want to say sorry to my younger self for being so harsh and so critical, and for not seeing what was already there.

And now I want to try something different. It's okay if someone doesn't see me the way I want to be seen. It's okay if I don't get that validation from others. As long as I can stand by myself and pick myself up, I'll be okay.

And for the first time, I feel like I actually have myself.

If you came across this, I hope you give yourself a break. Give yourself permission to believe that you are good enough. Because you actually are.
