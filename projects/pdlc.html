The Premise

You already know how this goes.

An idea lands. It feels right. You start building. Three weeks in, the scope has grown, the problem has shifted, and the thing you're building isn't quite what you meant to build. You finish it anyway. Or you don't.

Either way, you've learned what you should have known at the start. This pipeline moves that learning to before the first line of code. It starts from wherever you are: a clear idea, or nothing yet. It ends with a clickable prototype grounded in research, defined requirements, and a resolved UX spec. Everything in between is documented. Every stage is gated.

How It Runs

Six phases.
Five gates.

If you have an idea
Brainstorm Copilot
A conversational thinking partner. Does live web research before every response. Asks one question at a time. Challenges vague scope. Ends when the direction is clear: one idea, one platform, a scope, and at least one explicit not-building.
Gate 1 · Does this capture your idea?
If you don't have one
Discovery Agent
Four steps: define the person, surface pain points from real community signals, map what exists and where it falls short, then propose three idea candidates. Safe bet, unexpected angle, ambitious. You pick one.
Idea selected · Brainstorm is skipped entirely
Both paths arrive here
Structural formatter · silent
Intake Clarifier
Converts the handoff into a structured project brief. Runs invisibly between the idea step and everything else. The brief is frozen the moment it is written and never touched again.
project_brief.md
Vision writer
PRD Draft
Writes the first version of the product requirements from the brief alone, before any research runs. This is intentional. Capturing the raw vision first means research can genuinely test the idea rather than being reverse-engineered to justify it.
prd_draft.md
Adversarial analyst
Research
Four research tiers in parallel pairs. Direct competitors and community voice first, then industry signals and emerging patterns. Each tier cross-validates the others. The output is a compressed summary flagging only what the draft PRD got wrong, not the full report.
research.md
Gate 1.5 · Your call
Happy with the research?
Yes → continue. No → name what to dig into, and research reruns that section only, then Gate 1.5 again.
Evidence editor
PRD Refine
Reads the compressed research handoff. Updates only what the evidence actually changes. If the draft held up, it stays as written. Adds a User Snapshot: four lines synthesized from real research findings, not invented.
prd.md
Gate 2 · Your call
Requirements approved?
Yes → continue. Feedback → PRD reruns with targeted changes only, then Gate 2 again.
Experience designer
UX Research
Defines every user flow, information architecture, component state, and visual direction. If anything contradicts the approved requirements, it flags a conflict and routes back to the PRD agent. That loop runs until zero conflicts remain.
ux_research.md
Gate 3 · Hard lock
Does the full experience feel right?
Yes → requirements and UX lock together as the single source of truth. No scope changes, no flow changes after this point.
After Gate 3: the prototype is not a test of the design. It is an execution of it.
Builder
Prototype
Reads a compressed handoff from the locked UX spec. Generates a single HTML file that opens in any browser, no setup, no build step. Every must-have flow is navigable end to end. Concrete enough to get a real reaction, lightweight enough to throw away if needed.
prototype.html
Gate 4 · Your call
Does the prototype feel right?
Yes → pipeline complete. Visual or flow issue → UX reruns, Gate 3 reopens. Feature or scope issue → PRD reruns, then UX, then prototype.
What Makes It Different

Four principles that hold.

01
Research tests the vision. Not the other way around.
The draft requirements are written before research runs. That means findings are actually testing the idea rather than being reverse-engineered to support it.
02
Nothing moves without your sign-off.
Human approval gates sit between every phase. The pipeline never advances on its own. Autonomous work, deliberate handoffs.
03
UX conflicts are resolved before Gate 3. Never after.
Gate 3 only opens when zero conflicts remain, so nothing contradictory makes it into the prototype.
04
Agents pass summaries to each other, not documents.
Inter-agent communication stays lean so nothing gets hallucinated from context overload. Full documents exist for human review at gates.
Why I Built It

More ideas than systems.

I have more ideas than I have systems to act on them. I built this pipeline so that the moment I have an idea, I have somewhere to put it. Something that takes it where it needs to go without me having to invent the process every time.
