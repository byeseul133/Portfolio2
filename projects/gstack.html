The Premise

What does your own pipeline miss about itself?

The PDLC was built to take ideas through a structured process before anything gets built. But the pipeline itself was built without one. No forcing questions, no external review, no one pushing back on the assumptions baked in.

gstack is Garry Tan's open-source AI sprint toolkit: five skills, three phases, each one running a different kind of audit. I ran them against the PDLC the same way the PDLC runs against a product idea. The audit found six things the pipeline couldn't find about itself. Five of them were structural.

The Method

Three phases.
Five skills.

gstack's front-end sprint runs three phases in sequence.

구상 (Concept) — /office-hours produces a design document through six forcing questions.

기획 (Plan) — /plan-ceo-review, /plan-eng-review, and /plan-design-review run in parallel against that document, each from a different lens: strategy, architecture, design quality.

설계 (Design) — /design-consultation closes the phase with a complete design system.

Together they cover every angle the builder is too close to see.

What Changed

Six findings.
Five structural.

/office-hours · 구상

A pipeline that can't say no will always say yes

The pipeline can now stop. If research shows the idea doesn't hold up, it routes back to brainstorm — not forward to the plan.

If research proved the idea wrong, the old pipeline kept going anyway. The fix adds a real off-ramp. Gate 1 now also requires two questions before it opens: what already exists today, and what would make this idea not worth building?

Gate 1.5 · brainstorm.md · orchestrator.md
/plan-ceo-review · 기획

Research could agree with everything and no one would notice

Gate 1.5 now shows you exactly what the research changed in the plan — and flags it clearly if research changed nothing at all.

The plan is written before research runs on purpose. But there was no way to see how much the research actually challenged it. Now you can see the difference before moving on.

Gate 1.5 · prd.md · orchestrator.md
/plan-eng-review · 기획 · architecture

The UX conflict loop had no exit

The conflict resolution loop now stops after 3 rounds. If 3 rounds don't resolve it, the process pauses and a human decides what happens next.

Each fix could create a new conflict, and the loop had no way to end on its own. Three rounds is enough for real conflicts. If it takes more, the problem is bigger than the agents can handle.

UX Research → Gate 3 loop · ux_research.md
/plan-eng-review · 기획 · failure modes

The deepest research layer was skipped automatically

You now choose how deep the research goes. Thorough is the default. Skipping the deepest layer is a decision you make explicitly.

The old system would quietly skip the deepest research layer if it found nothing in the first two tries. That layer is where the most useful signals tend to live. Every research output now also requires a Failure Modes section.

Research Agent · research.md
/plan-design-review · 기획

The pipeline had agents. It didn't have a team.

Every agent now has a name: Structural formatter, Vision writer, Adversarial analyst, Evidence editor, Experience designer, Builder.

A label tells you what something makes. A name tells you how to read what it makes — and how much to push back on it.

All 6 agent specs · brainstorm.md · prd.md · research.md · ux_research.md · prototype.md
/design-consultation · 설계

Every prototype made its own visual decisions from scratch

Every prototype now starts from the same visual rulebook. One file — DESIGN.md — captures the design decisions once.

The audit didn't change what the pipeline looks like. It wrote down what was already there and made it reusable. Now adding a new feature means deciding whether it fits what already exists.

What It Validated

Three things came back untouched.

Not everything changed. The two-file output system confirmed as correct architecture. The handoff format — summaries between agents, not full documents — confirmed as the right call for context management. Gate 3 as a hard lock confirmed: any pipeline that allows scope changes after the UX spec is approved will always find a reason to make one more change.
